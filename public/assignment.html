UI/UX design: creating your project idea brief

Now that you have chosen your final project ideas, the next step is to create an idea brief for each of your projects using Google Docs.

This brief should be thorough enough to allow us to understand your vision for the final product. This is crucial for the next stages of design and development. Please follow these guidelines while creating your idea brief:

  • Project Brief Description: Write a concise and clear description of your project. This should include what the project is about, its purpose, the problem it is trying to solve, and how it intends to solve it.
  • Target Audience: Identify the general group of people who are intended to use your application. While a user persona is not required at this stage, you should at least know the age range, occupations, and other general characteristics of your prospective users.
  • User Types: Enumerate the different types of users who will be interacting with your app. For example, in a medical app, there might be patients and doctors as separate user types.
  • Features of the App: For each user type, list the features of the app in user stories form. A user story is a simple way to capture a description of a product feature from an end-user perspective. An example would be: "As a [User Type], I want [the feature or functionality] so that [reason/benefit]."
  • Additional Information: Include any other details that might help me understand the app better. This could include the unique selling proposition, key differentiators, competitor analysis, potential challenges, etc.

Upon completion, please share your Google Doc with me by providing access (email: julien@sefactory.io). Ensure the sharing settings allow me to edit the document. Submit the link in Google Classroom under the appropriate assignment by midnight tonight.
